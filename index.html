About this page

I built this website to make a general resource for the history of Fascism as it existed until roughly 1939. The point of this website is to be an educational tool about historical Fascism: its belief systems, its political strategies, why it came to dominate some governments across the world, and how it failed. Another additional purpose for this website is for it to be an academically rigourous study in Fascism which is easily available to the public. Most academic discussion about Fascism is locked behind expensive books or paywalled academic journals. That's not necessarily a bad thing - those historians need to get paid somehow - but it would be good to have more resources freely available too. This is my attempt, as a historian with a university degree, to bring information that's normally locked behind those paywalls into a more public view. My general hope is that by learning about the Fascisms of the past we can be better prepared to combat Fascism in the present day and in the future.

This webpage was coded from scratch. No website creation tools or AI text generators were used to build it.

Important Disclaimer

Fascism is almost as controversial a topic today as it was during its hayday in the 1930s. There has been a general dilution of the term "Fascist" into a meaningless term thrown around as an insult at political parties on all parts of the political spectrum. This page's scope is specifically about the Fascist movements that existed in the 1900s. Present-day Fascists share the same ideological framework as Mussolini and Hitler, but not always the exact same beliefs or tactics. Basing all your knowledge of Fascism from its 20th century beliefs runs the danger of being blind to "Neo-Fascism" as it exists in the 21st century. There is a recommended reading section on modern "Neo-Fascist" movements on this website - do not take 20th century Fascism as your be-all-end-all understanding of 21st century Fascism.
