1909 Publication of Manifesto of Futurism The Manifesto of Futurism, written by Filippo Tommaso Marinetti, laid out the basic tenents of the growing right-wing beleif of Futurism. The belief became an important part of Fascist ideology, and Marinetti would himself co-author the original Fascist manifesto in 1919. Marinetti eventually became disgusted with the Fascist cause and left, but his work continued to influence the development of the Fascist party.

1914 Beginning of World War One In August of 1914 Austria declared war on Serbia, which spun into an international incident that would result in the Entente, made up of Britain, France, and Russia, being at war with the Central Powers, composed of Germany, Austria, and the Ottoman Empire.
1915 Italian entry into World War One The Italians resisted joining the war, largely due to a large and organized anti-war movement in the country. Italy joined the Entente after being promised land gains in the Balkans.

1918 End of World War One In November 1918 the war ended with a ceasefire. In 1919 the Central Powers signed the Treaty of Versailles with the Entente powers. Italy got much less territory in the treaty than they had been promised when they joined the war.
1919 Founding of the Italian Fascist Party

Italian elections		 Following gatherings of fasci, or groups, of interventionist Italians, Mussolini founded the Fascist party in 1919.

The first elections in Italy to feature universal male suffrage were held in 1919. The two parties that performed best were the Italian Socialist Party and the left-wing-Catholic Italian Popular Party.
